> Governance

Inference is admitted, suppressed, or refused

Neraium does not treat every anomaly as meaningful. Governance defines when the system may infer engineering significance, when weak signals must be suppressed, and when uncertainty should override escalation entirely.

[Governance Components]

Admissibility Boundary

Interpretation is permitted only when system behaviour meets defined admissibility conditions rather than merely exhibiting statistical irregularity.

  • Physical interpretation constraints
  • Temporal persistence requirements
  • Cross-signal coherence checks
  • Boundary enforcement before escalation

Evidence Preservation

Every admitted outcome is retained with the context needed for later technical, operational, and institutional review.

  • Measured system divergence
  • Criteria applied at evaluation time
  • System state during assessment
  • Final interpretation outcome

[Decision Outcomes]

When Signals Are Admitted

Persistent Deviation

The observed pattern remains present long enough to exceed transient anomaly behaviour.

Physical Plausibility

The interpretation remains consistent with engineering-valid system behaviour.

Cross-Signal Agreement

Multiple signals support the interpretation instead of contradicting it.

Escalation Eligibility

Only after admissibility is satisfied can a finding advance into reviewable output.

When Signals Are Blocked

  • Suppressed: weak, transient, or poorly supported deviations do not advance to meaningful interpretation.
  • Refused: signals that fall outside valid interpretive boundaries are denied inference altogether.
  • Bounded: the system cannot claim more meaning than the available evidence and constraints permit.
  • Reviewable: the basis for admission, suppression, or refusal remains available for later inspection.

[Governance Role]

Governance exists to preserve interpretive discipline. It prevents statistically unusual behaviour from being automatically treated as meaningful system knowledge and ensures that any inference advanced by the platform remains bounded, defensible, and open to review.

[Accountability Model]

Neraium preserves the context required to understand why a signal was admitted, suppressed, or refused. That record supports engineering analysis, post-incident review, regulatory scrutiny, and institutional memory.
