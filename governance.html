Governance

Infrastructure intelligence with clear operational boundaries

Neraium is designed as a read-only interpretation layer. It provides visibility into system behavior without interfering with operational authority, automation logic, or human control.

View Principles
Principles

Operational principles that define the system boundary

The governance model exists to keep interpretation disciplined, transparent, and explicitly separated from control.

Read-Only Architecture

The platform observes infrastructure and telemetry but never issues commands or performs actuation.

Operational Independence

Existing control systems remain unchanged and continue to own direct authority over the environment.

Infrastructure Transparency

The environment becomes measurable and explainable without forcing hidden or opaque decision paths.

Accountability

All surfaced intelligence remains auditable through recorded logic, constraints, and outcomes.

Boundaries

System boundaries are explicit

Neraium operates outside the control environment. Infrastructure authority remains fully with existing systems and with the people responsible for operational decisions.

The platform may interpret telemetry and preserve evidence, but it does not assume operational authority. Human decision making remains the final layer of action.

Governance Architecture

How information moves through the governance model

The architecture is intentionally simple: infrastructure generates telemetry, Neraium interprets that telemetry, and humans remain the final decision point.

Infrastructure Systems

Operational assets generate raw state and behaviour.

Telemetry Signals

Signals are exported into an observation layer.

Neraium Analysis Layer

Signals are evaluated under deterministic interpretive constraints.

Operational Intelligence

Only admissible, documented interpretations move forward.

Human Decision Making

Action remains outside the platform and with the operator.
