Governance

Operational principles for a read-only intelligence layer

Neraium operates as a read-only infrastructure intelligence layer that provides system visibility without modifying operational control, replacing human authority, or crossing system boundaries.

View Principles
Principles

Four governance commitments

The governance framework exists to ensure that Neraium remains interpretable, auditable, and subordinate to existing operational authority.

Read-Only Architecture

The platform observes infrastructure systems without issuing commands, changing state, or performing actuation.

Operational Independence

Existing control systems remain unchanged and continue to retain direct authority over operational environments.

Infrastructure Transparency

Operational conditions become measurable and explainable through interpretable system intelligence.

Accountability

Every surfaced interpretation remains reviewable through preserved logic, evidence, and contextual recordkeeping.

System Boundaries

Authority remains outside the platform

Neraium is designed to interpret telemetry and preserve evidence. It is not designed to assume control authority, replace operator judgment, or override operational systems.

Infrastructure authority remains with existing systems and with the human operators responsible for acting on conditions in the environment. Neraium informs. Operators decide.

Governance Architecture

How information moves through the model

The governance structure is deliberately simple. Systems generate telemetry, Neraium interprets that telemetry under explicit constraints, and humans remain the final decision point.

Infrastructure Systems

Operational assets generate raw system state and behavior.

Telemetry Signals

Signals are exported into an observation layer for analysis.

Neraium Analysis Layer

Telemetry is evaluated under deterministic interpretive constraints.

Operational Intelligence

Only admissible, documented interpretations move forward.

Human Decision Making

Action remains external to the platform and with the operator.
