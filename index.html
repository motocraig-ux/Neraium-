What Neraium does

Detects developing instability across systems, not just out-of-range signals

Neraium evaluates how signals move together over time: load vs temperature, flow vs pressure, speed vs vibration, energy vs output. When those relationships shift, it can indicate degradation or instability while individual tags remain “normal.”

Early-change detections

Time-bounded findings indicating where relationships are drifting or becoming unstable.

Reviewable evidence

Signal sets, windows, and relationship shifts that can be examined by engineering teams.

Practical triage

What changed, when it started, and which subsystems are implicated, to guide inspection and diagnosis.

Why not threshold-only monitoring?

Conventional alarms are late when the failure mode is structural

Most monitoring is built around individual tag limits. But many industrial degradations show up first as a change in relationships between tags, long before any one value is clearly abnormal.

  • Changing relationship between power, load, and temperature
  • Cooling performance drifting relative to operating conditions
  • Unstable dynamics across coupled subsystems
  • Mechanical behavior changing under similar load conditions

How Neraium works

Tracks changes in relationships between signals over time

Neraium builds a baseline of how telemetry behaves together under varying conditions, then continuously tests for structural drift. These changes in the geometry of signal relationships can indicate developing instability.

Plain-English

Instead of asking “is this tag high?”, it asks “are these tags still behaving together the way they used to?”

Technical

It evaluates multivariate structure and its change over time, focusing on relationship stability rather than isolated threshold exceedance.
