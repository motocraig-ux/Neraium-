Overview

Turning maintenance from reactive to proactive

Industrial facilities generate enormous amounts of sensor data, yet many equipment failures still appear to happen unexpectedly. In reality, failures are usually preceded by subtle changes in how different parts of the system behave together.

Neraium provides Systemic Infrastructure Intelligence, an approach that analyzes how signals across an industrial system interact over time. When these relationships begin to shift, it can indicate that degradation or instability is emerging even when individual sensors still appear normal.

The Problem

Why conventional monitoring misses early change

Most monitoring systems evaluate equipment using thresholds or anomaly detection on individual sensors. These tools work well for obvious issues but often miss the early stages of developing problems.

  • Changing relationships between power, load, and temperature
  • Cooling performance drifting relative to operating conditions
  • Unstable process dynamics across multiple subsystems
  • Mechanical systems behaving differently under varying loads

These signals can be difficult to detect when sensors are analyzed individually. Neraium focuses on identifying changes in system behavior itself.

Approach

A new intelligence layer for industrial infrastructure

Neraium analyzes industrial systems as networks of interacting signals. The platform continuously evaluates relationships between sensors, stability of system behavior over time, emerging patterns that indicate degradation, and shifts in operating dynamics.

When meaningful structural changes appear, the system highlights the equipment or processes that may require attention. This allows reliability teams to move from reactive maintenance to proactive intervention.
