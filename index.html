How it works

Four steps from baseline to early warning

Neraium learns how healthy systems behave, measures drift in those relationships, confirms persistent instability through multiple gates, and delivers lead time before visible failure.

1. Learn baseline

Model how signals relate during normal operation across all operating regimes.

2. Measure drift

Detect when signal relationships deviate from baseline patterns.

3. Confirm instability

Apply persistence, accumulation, and post-baseline validation to rule out noise.

4. Give lead time

Alert operators with signal-level diagnostics weeks to cycles before failure.

Why structural detection comes first

Thresholds confirm failure, structure predicts it

Traditional alarms trigger when measurements cross preset limits. Structural instability detection identifies when the relationships between signals begin to degrade, which typically precedes any single threshold breach by weeks or cycles.
