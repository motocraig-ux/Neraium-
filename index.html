Structural Shift

As physical infrastructure becomes increasingly instrumented, intelligence expands toward operational authority. In high-consequence environments, analysis must remain separate from control. That separation becomes a structural requirement.

Drift Detection, Not Threshold Alarms

High-consequence water systems rarely fail without precursors. The precursor is not a single sensor crossing a limit. It is sustained multivariate drift across correlated signals over time.

Multivariate drift

Detect persistent deviation across temperature, flow, pressure, and energy signals, not isolated spikes.

Persistence confirmation

Classify deterioration only when deviation persists, reducing noise and preventing reactive false positives.

Lead time framing

Translate drift onset into lead time against downstream alarms, failures, or consequence events.

Technical Box
Inputs: native telemetry streams Baseline: stable operating window Detection: multivariate distance and correlation shift Confirmation: persistence and consistency checks Output: deterioration class + onset timestamp + lead time

Why it matters
