The Problem

Why reactive systems fail

Infrastructure failures are rarely sudden. They emerge from long, subtle periods of multivariate drift that threshold-based systems and opaque predictive models often miss.

Reactive systems

Thresholds trigger late, sensors are evaluated in isolation, and operators are left without meaningful system context.

Neraium

Structural deviation is surfaced early, system behaviour becomes explainable, and decisions are supported by defensible records.

Technical Foundation

Built on structured system interpretation

Neraium evaluates system behaviour through multivariate geometry, deterministic gating, and evidence-first logging. It is designed to refuse weak interpretations rather than invent certainty.

Signal Geometry Layer

Models telemetry as a multivariate system, enabling structural drift detection without depending on failure-history datasets.

Aletheia’s Gate

Applies rule-based constraints such as persistence, physical legitimacy, and multi-sensor agreement before surfacing an interpretation.

Evidence Vault

Records whether a signal was admitted, suppressed, or voided, preserving a permanent trail for post-event review.
