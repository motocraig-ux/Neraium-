How systems actually fail

Alarms trigger too late.

When threshold alerts fire, degradation is already advanced. You have minutes to respond, not hours or days. Emergency response becomes your only option.

You see a symptom, not the cause.

A pressure spike. A temperature deviation. You know something changed. You do not know what broke or why. Triage takes time you do not have.

Failures feel sudden.

They are not. Your systems degraded gradually. Components stopped working together the way they should. You just could not see it happening.

What Systemic Infrastructure Intelligence is

Systems work as a whole. Their components interact. Problems show up in relationships first, long before they appear in any single signal. Systemic Infrastructure Intelligence finds those relationships before they break.

Validated on real failure data

NASA CMAPSS FD004 contains turbofan engine telemetry from engines run to failure. We tested Neraium against this dataset with known failure times and confirmed degradation patterns.

Earlier detection

Neraium consistently detected degradation patterns before conventional threshold alerts triggered on FD004 data.

40 to 160 cycles lead time

In FD004 testing, this provided 40 to 160 cycles before failure. The range reflects different failure modes within the dataset.
