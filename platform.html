> Platform

A read-only platform for systemic infrastructure interpretation

Neraium sits alongside existing infrastructure systems, ingests telemetry without modifying control environments, and produces structured interpretation for engineering review and operational awareness.

[Platform Layers]

[01]

Telemetry Intake Layer

Receives multivariate telemetry from historians, SCADA environments, edge systems, and other existing operational data sources without requiring replacement of incumbent infrastructure.

[02]

Interpretation Layer

Evaluates structural relationships across signals to determine whether system behaviour remains coherent over time, rather than treating measurements as isolated alarm conditions.

[03]

Review Output Layer

Produces operator-facing and engineering-facing outputs that support review, escalation, and downstream accountability without issuing machine control commands.

[Operational Role]

Neraium operates as an interpretation layer, not a control layer. It reads telemetry, evaluates systemic behaviour, and surfaces structured findings for human and institutional use. It does not actuate equipment, modify PLC logic, or replace established supervisory systems.

[Deployment Model]

The platform is designed for edge-oriented deployment, allowing telemetry to be processed close to the operational environment. This supports lower-latency analysis, preserves local control boundaries, and reduces dependence on continuous cloud connectivity.

[Platform Characteristics]

System Fit

Structured Outputs

Why It Matters

  • Low-friction adoption: designed to work with infrastructure that already exists in the field.
  • Operational separation: preserves a clear boundary between interpretation and machine control.
  • Deployment flexibility: supports infrastructure environments where connectivity and trust boundaries vary.
  • Engineering usability: surfaces findings in a form that can be reviewed, challenged, and acted on responsibly.
