Core Methods

How the platform models behavior

Neraium does not depend on isolated thresholding or purely black-box forecasting. It models system structure directly through relationships between variables.

Mahalanobis Distance

Measures system deviation in a multivariate space so correlated signals are treated as part of one system rather than as separate readings.

Covariance Modeling

Builds a representation of how telemetry variables move together, enabling structural changes in behavior to be detected directly.

Adaptive Baselines

Maintains operational relevance by refreshing the expected system structure from recent valid behavior.

Interpretation Constraints

Detection is not enough

Candidate signals must pass deterministic constraints before an interpretation is admitted. The system is designed to suppress ambiguity rather than translate it into false certainty.

Physical Validity

Signals must remain inside plausible physical interpretation boundaries or they are treated as invalid for operational reasoning.

Temporal Persistence

Deviation must persist long enough to indicate meaningful structural change rather than transient noise.

Multi-Sensor Agreement

Interpretable signals require corroboration across related telemetry rather than relying on one channel in isolation.

Evidence Logic

Every outcome becomes part of the record

Neraium records what was admitted, suppressed, or voided along with the system context and applied logic. This makes interpretation traceable and reviewable after the fact.

Admitted Signals

Signals that satisfy deviation and interpretability thresholds are preserved as operational evidence.

Suppressed Signals

Candidate deviations that fail physical or logical constraints are recorded without being surfaced as valid interpretations.

Void Outcomes

Conditions that do not exceed relevant thresholds are treated as non-events and remain structurally accounted for.
